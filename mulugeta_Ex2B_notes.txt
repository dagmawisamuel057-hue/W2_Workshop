For Lana’s dog-walking business, our group identified four main entities: Customers, Pets, Walkers, and Walks.

Customers stores owner information such as customer ID, first name, last name, phone, email, and address.

Pets stores information about each dog, including pet ID, customer ID, pet name, breed, age, and notes. This table connects each pet to its owner.

Walkers stores staff information, including walker ID, walker name, phone, and email.

Walks stores each dog-walking appointment, including walk ID, pet ID, walker ID, walk date, walk time, duration, status, and payment amount.

Relationships

One customer can have many pets.
One pet can have many walks.
One walker can complete many walks.
Each walk is linked to one pet and one walker.

This structure reduces duplicate data and makes it easier to track customers, dogs, employees, and appointments.
